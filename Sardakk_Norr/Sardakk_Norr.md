## I. Introduction

The Sardakk N'orr are TI4's purest combat faction. This faction excels at winning fights through Unrelenting (+1 to all combat rolls), leveraging superior ground forces and dreadnoughts (Exotrireme with BOMBARDMENT 4 x2 + Capacity 1), and extracting value from every victory. Sardakk isn't about economic efficiency or technological supremacyâ€”it's about military dominance, winning through superior combat math, and turning aggression into victory points.

The payoff? Watching opponents realize your infantry hit on 7 instead of 8 (+1 Unrelenting), seeing your Exotrireme dreadnoughts BOMBARD at 4 with 2 dice then land infantry via capacity 1, and using Valkyrie Particle Weave to generate extra hits whenever opponents hit you. When you've mastered combat math and leverage your +1 advantage perfectly, every battle becomes a favorable trade.

## II. Playstyle

Playing Sardakk N'orr is like being a relentless war machine. Your Unrelenting gives +1 to ALL combat rolls (space combat, ground combat, BOMBARDMENT, SPACE CANNON, AFB), your Exotrireme dreadnoughts have built-in capacity for invasions, and your breakthrough (N'orr Supremacy) lets you gain CCs or research unit upgrades after winning combats. You're not turtling or tech-racingâ€”you're fighting constantly and converting victories into tangible advantages.

The key strength of Sardakk is combat superiority without tech investment. Your +1 Unrelenting makes all your units better than opponents' units at the same tech level. Infantry hit on 7 (vs 8), fighters hit on 8 (vs 9), dreadnoughts hit on 4 (vs 5). This scalesâ€”even with 0 technologies researched, you're competitive in combat.

Opponents will fear engaging you in combat once they realize your math advantage. That moment when your "basic" infantry consistently defeat their "upgraded" ground forces? Pure military dominance.

### E. Leaders

**Agent - T'ro:**

At the end of a player's tactical ACTION: You may exhaust this card; if you do, that player may place 2 infantry from their reinforcements on a planet they control in the active system.

Support agent. After any tactical action, exhaust to let that player (you or opponent) place 2 free infantry on planets in active system. Use on yourself (free reinforcements) or trade to others for favors.

**Commander - G'hom Sek'kus:** *Unlock: Control 5 planets in non-home systems.*

During the "Commit Ground Forces" step: You can commit up to 1 ground force from each planet in the active system and each planet in adjacent systems that do not contain 1 of your command tokens.

Powerful invasion commander. Easy unlock (5 non-home planets by R2-R3). Once unlocked, when invading, you can commit ground forces from:
1. Planets in the active system (normal)
2. Planets in ADJACENT systems (not normal!)

Devastating invasion hero. Move ships into system, skip directly to invasion (no space combat), land ground forces, then your ships disappear. Use this to:
1. Bypass enemy fleets entirely (no space combat)
2. Invade multiple planets with ground forces
3. Sacrifice ships (they return to reinforcements, not destroyed)

Example: Move 3 carriers with 12 infantry into enemy system. Hero: Skip space combat, land 12 infantry on 3 planets, ships disappear. You just invaded 3 planets without fighting their fleet.

**Summary:**

## IV. Structural Weaknesses

### A. First Turn Priorities

Your R1 priority order: **Warfare > Leadership > Technology**

Focus on:
1. **Warfare** - Enables aggression and fleet redistribution.
2. **Leadership** - CCs for expansion and combat.
3. **Technology** - Begin tech path (you have 0 techs, desperately need to catch up).
4. **Trade** - Refresh 3 commodities for resources.

**Expansion Notes:** You have 2 carriers (capacity 8), 1 cruiser, and 5 infantry. Excellent R1 expansion capability. Aim for 3-4 systems R1, prioritizing weak neighbors or contested equidistant systems. Your 5 infantry give you ground force superiority R1.

### B. Technology Deficit

0 starting technologies means you're behind everyone R1. You need to research aggressively R2-R4 OR rely on N'orr Supremacy (win combats to research unit upgrades).

**Mitigation:** Use breakthrough to research unit upgrades from combat wins. Fight 3-5 times R2-R4, research 3-5 unit upgrades without taking Technology. Alternatively, take Technology R2-R3 to catch up.

### C. Political Weakness

Only 1 influence at home makes you terrible at voting and political objectives. Expand to influence planets quickly.

**Mitigation:** Expand to cultural planets R1-R2. Use military strength to intimidate political opponents. Focus on combat objectives, not political ones.

### D. Over-Aggression Risk

Sardakk players often over-commit to combat, exhausting their fleets and leaving themselves vulnerable to counterattacks. You're strong in combat but not immortal.

**Mitigation:** Fight selectively. Target weak opponents or contested systems. Don't burn all your fleet fighting a strong military factionâ€”raid weak economic factions instead.

## V. Technology

### A. Overview

You start with **0 technologies.**

Your main tech path should leverage N'orr Supremacy (research unit upgrades from combat wins) or traditional tech paths focusing on unit upgrades and mobility.

### B. Tech Path 1: Combat Supremacy (Standard)

**Starting Techs:** None (0)

**Round 1: Sarween Tools (Y)**
- When you use PRODUCTION, reduce combined cost by 1
- **Why:** Economic efficiency. You'll produce constantly (dreadnoughts, infantry).
- **Prerequisites:** None (0)

**Round 2: Exotrireme II (BBY) - WITH N'ORR SUPREMACY**
- N'orr Dreadnought: Cost 4 | Combat 5 | Move 2 | Capacity 1 | BOMBARDMENT 4 (x2) | Sustain Damage | Cannot be destroyed by Direct Hit | After combat round, may destroy this to destroy up to 2 ships
- **Why:** Your faction tech. Upgraded dreadnoughts with move 2, Direct Hit immunity, and kamikaze ability (destroy self to kill 2 ships).
- **Prerequisites:** 2 blue + 1 yellow (Sarween + 2 blue techs OR win combat with N'orr Supremacy to bypass)
- **Via Breakthrough:** Win 1 combat, research Exotrireme II directly

**Round 3: Valkyrie Particle Weave (RR) - WITH N'ORR SUPREMACY**
- After making combat rolls in ground combat, if opponent produced hits, you produce 1 additional hit
- **Why:** Your other faction tech. Generates extra hits in ground combat when you're hit.
- **Prerequisites:** 2 red (need red techs OR win combat with N'orr Supremacy)
- **Via Breakthrough:** Win 1 combat, research Valkyrie Particle Weave directly

**Round 4: Gravity Drive (BB)**
- +1 move to 1 ship per activation
- **Why:** Mobility for raids and repositioning.

**Round 5+:** Carrier II, Fleet Logistics, other flex techs

**Tech Requirements:**
- **Combat victories (CRITICAL for breakthrough path):** Win 3-5 combats R2-R4 to research both faction techs + unit upgrades
- **Blue skip (helpful):** Accelerates Exotrireme II if not using breakthrough
- **Yellow skip (helpful):** Speeds Sarween
- **Red skip (helpful):** Accelerates Valkyrie Particle Weave

**Pros:**
- Uses N'orr Supremacy to research from combat wins (no Technology needed)
- Both faction techs by R3
- Aggressive tech progression
- Constant fighting = constant tech gains

**Cons:**
- Requires winning combats (risky if opponents avoid you)

**Best For:** Aggressive games with weak neighbors. Fight constantly, research from victories.

- Score combat objectives (Spark a Rebellion, Darken the Skies, etc.)

**Key Strategic Principles:**
1. **Fight constantly** - Every combat win = CC or unit upgrade via N'orr Supremacy
