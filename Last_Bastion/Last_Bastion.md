# The Last Bastion Comprehensive Strategy Guide

## I. Introduction

The Last Bastion are Twilight Imperium's **escalating combat faction**, rewarding sustained warfare through the unique **Galvanize** mechanic. Each combat allows you to galvanize units (placing tokens beneath them that grant +1 die for all combat rolls), creating a snowball effect where your fleet becomes progressively stronger with each engagement. Your Liberate ability readies planets instantly if you invade with infantry equal to the planet's resource value, accelerating economic recovery. You start with a choice of any blue or yellow technology with no prerequisites, control the legendary Ordinian planet (exhaust when passing for action card + command token), and deploy unique Helios space docks that increase planet resources. This faction rewards aggressive expansion, frequent combat to stack galvanize tokens, and leveraging your scaling flagship (+1 combat per planet you control) to dominate late-game.

## II. Playstyle

**Early Game (Rounds 1-2):**
Your opening focuses on selecting your starting technology strategically (Gravity Drive, Fleet Logistics, or Sarween Tools recommended) and leveraging Liberate for rapid expansion. When conquering planets, bring infantry equal to the planet's resource value to ready it immediately, allowing you to produce units the same round. Your Phoenix Standard ability galvanizes 1 unit after each combat, so seek early conflicts (even small skirmishes) to start accumulating galvanize tokens. Your unique Helios space docks increase planet resources by +1, making even 1-resource planets into 2-resource production hubs. Your Ordinian legendary planet provides insurance: exhaust it when passing to gain 1 action card + 1 command token if you need resources late in a round. With only 1 commodity, trade economy is nonexistent‚Äîfocus on conquest.

**Mid Game (Rounds 3-4):**
This is when galvanize tokens snowball. Your commander (Nip and Tuck) unlocks with only 3 galvanized units on board, granting immunity to Sabotage action cards and Nekro assimilation. Continue seeking combats to galvanize units; each galvanized unit rolls +1 die, making your fleets disproportionately strong. Your flagship (The Egeiro) gains +1 combat for each non-home planet you control, scaling massively with territorial expansion. Build more Helios space docks on conquered planets to increase their resource values further (V2 upgrade grants +2 resources). Your Proxima Targeting faction tech cancels 1 bombardment hit per galvanized ground force, making your invasions highly resistant to bombardment. Research The Icon breakthrough (yellow/red) to produce ships directly in systems with your command tokens and ground forces, enabling rapid reinforcement.

**Late Game (Rounds 5-6+):**
By this stage, you should have 6-10 galvanized units forming an unstoppable armada. Your hero (Entity 4X41A "Apollo") triggers when a galvanized unit dies, rolling dice to destroy ALL enemy units in that system (each die that meets combat value destroys a unit). This creates devastating board clears when your galvanized dreadnoughts or cruisers die. Your flagship with 8-10 planets controlled rolls at combat 17-19 (effectively auto-hitting everything), making it nearly invincible. The Icon breakthrough lets you produce entire fleets in forward positions, bypassing space dock placement. Victory comes from **sustained territorial control**, leveraging galvanize scaling to win combats effortlessly, and scoring combat/territorial objectives.

## III. The Basics

### A. Home System & Commodities

**Ordinian (Home System - Legendary):** 0 resources, 0 influence
- A legendary planet with NO economic value
- **Legendary Ability:** *You may exhaust this card when you pass to draw 1 action card and gain 1 command token*
- Critical insurance mechanism: if you passed early, exhaust for action card + token
- Provides late-round flexibility
- 0/0 means you rely entirely on expansion for economy

**Revelation (Home System - Trade Station):** 1 resource, 2 influence
- Small resource/influence contribution
- Trade Station provides minor economic boost
- 2 influence helps early agenda phases slightly
- Combined with Ordinian, you start with very weak economy

**Commodities:** 1
- THE LOWEST commodity count in the entire game
- Trade economy is completely non-viable
- Other factions won't prioritize trading with you
- Focus entirely on conquest for economic growth

**Optimal Resource/Influence from Home Worlds:**
- Ordinian: 0 optimal resources / 0 optimal influence (legendary ability is the value)
- Revelation: 1 optimal resource / 2 optimal influence
- Total: 1 optimal resource / 2 optimal influence from home
- You DESPERATELY need expansion for any economic viability

### D. Faction Abilities

**Liberate:**
*When you gain control of a planet, ready that planet if it contains a number of your infantry equal to or greater than that planet's resource value; otherwise, place 1 infantry on that planet.*

- Conquer with infantry ‚â• planet resources ‚Üí planet readies immediately
- Otherwise, gain 1 free infantry on that planet
- Example: 3-resource planet conquered with 3+ infantry ‚Üí ready immediately, can produce units
- Example: 3-resource planet conquered with 2 infantry ‚Üí planet exhausted, gain 1 infantry (now 3 total)
- Encourages infantry-heavy invasions
- Readied planets = immediate production capability = snowball expansion
- Plan invasions with enough infantry to trigger ready effect

**Galvanize:**
*When a game effect instructs a player to galvanize a unit, they place a galvanize token beneath it if it does not have one. Galvanized units roll 1 additional die for combat rolls and unit abilities.*

- Galvanized units gain +1 die for combat and unit abilities
- Only 1 galvanize token per unit (cannot stack multiple tokens)
- Applies to space combat, ground combat, bombardment, anti-fighter barrage, space cannon
- Permanent until unit is destroyed
- Your faction abilities, agent, commander, and hero all interact with galvanize
- Galvanize tokens stay even if unit is damaged

**Phoenix Standard:**
*At the end of a combat, you may galvanize 1 of your units that participated.*

- After EVERY combat (space or ground), galvanize 1 participating unit
- You choose which unit to galvanize
- Prioritize high-value units: dreadnoughts, flagship, cruisers (multi-die units benefit less)
- Encourages frequent combat to accumulate galvanize tokens
- Even losing combats lets you galvanize survivors
- Galvanize units that will fight repeatedly

### E. Leaders

**Agent: Dame Briar**
*When a player's unit is destroyed: You may exhaust this card to galvanize another of that player's units in the destroyed unit's system.*

- Exhausts when ANY player's unit is destroyed
- Galvanizes ANOTHER unit belonging to that player in the same system
- Can use on yourself: when your unit dies, galvanize a survivor
- Can use on other players: transaction tool (galvanize their unit for support)
- High value for defensive combats: lose 1 fighter, galvanize your dreadnought
- Moderate transaction value

**Commander: Nip and Tuck**
*Unlock: There are 3 galvanized units on the game board.*
*Your action cards cannot be canceled by "Sabotage" action cards. The Nekro Virus cannot place assimilator tokens on your components.*

- Unlocks naturally through Phoenix Standard (galvanize 1 unit per combat)
- Extremely powerful against Nekro and action-card-heavy metagames
- Unlock by Round 2-3 easily

**Hero: Entity 4X41A "Apollo"**
*Unlock: Have 3 scored objectives.*
*Intelligence Unshackled:*
*When one of your galvanized units is destroyed: You may purge this card to roll 1 die for each unit in its system that belongs to another player; if the result is equal to or greater than the galvanized unit's combat value, destroy that unit.*

- Triggers when YOUR galvanized unit is destroyed
- Roll 1 die per ENEMY unit in that system
- Each die result ‚â• galvanized unit's combat value = destroy that enemy unit
- Example: Galvanized dreadnought (combat 5) dies; roll 8 dice for 8 enemy units; each 5+ destroys a unit
- Devastating board clear potential
- Best used with high-combat-value galvanized units (dreadnoughts at 5, flagship at 9+)
- Opponent must destroy your galvanized unit to trigger
- Can backfire if unit has low combat value (fighters at 9 require 9+ rolls)

### F. Promissory Note

## IV. Structural Weaknesses

**1. Worst Commodity Economy in the Game:**
You have only 1 commodity‚Äîthe absolute lowest in TI4. Trade strategy card is nearly worthless for you, and other factions won't prioritize trading with you. Economic growth depends entirely on conquest and Helios space dock resource bonuses. You cannot compete economically with trade factions (Hacan, Nomad, Winnu).

**2. Weak Starting Economy:**
With 1/2 from home and 1 commodity, you begin with the weakest economy in the game. Early rounds require aggressive expansion to acquire resources, but you lack the economic foundation to support large fleets or expensive technologies. You'll struggle to fund both fleet production and technology research simultaneously.

**3. Commander Unlock Dependency:**
While unlocking your commander (3 galvanized units) is easy, it requires seeking combats. Against passive opponents or in peaceful early games, you may struggle to accumulate galvanize tokens. If you cannot trigger combats Rounds 1-3, your scaling is delayed.

**4. Galvanize Snowball Requires Combats:**
Your entire power curve depends on fighting frequently. Against avoidance strategies (Ghosts teleporting away, Naalu using initiative to dodge fights, Nomad retreating), you cannot galvanize units and lose your faction's primary advantage. Peaceful metagames weaken Last Bastion significantly.

**5. Infantry Sustainability for Liberate:**
Liberate requires infantry equal to planet resources. High-resource planets (4-5 resources) need 4-5 infantry invasions, draining your ground forces. Without Sarween Tools or infantry production, you'll struggle to maintain Liberate-ready invasion forces across multiple conquests.

**6. Hero Timing Challenges:**
Your hero (Apollo) requires a galvanized unit to die, meaning you must lose a valuable unit to trigger the board clear. Opponents can play around this by avoiding destroying your galvanized units or focusing non-galvanized units first. Timing the hero trigger optimally is difficult.

**7. Flagship Scaling Takes Time:**
Your flagship scales with non-home planets controlled. Early game (2-4 planets), it's only combat 11-13. Mid-game (5-7 planets), combat 14-16. Only late-game (8+ planets) does it become truly dominant at combat 17+. You must survive long enough to reach late-game.

**8. Reliance on Starting Technology Choice:**
Your Round 1 technology choice (blue or yellow with no prerequisites) is permanent. If you choose poorly (e.g., Scanners instead of Fleet Logistics), you'll struggle all game. There's no recovery from a suboptimal starting tech decision.

- High-risk, high-reward bombardment

- Enables rapid reinforcement of frontlines

**Fleet Logistics (Blue):**
*During each of your turns in the action phase, you may perform 2 actions instead of 1.*

- 2 tactical actions per round
- Essential for competitive play
- If not chosen as starting tech, research Round 2-3 minimum

**Sarween Tools (Yellow):**
*When 1 or more of your units use PRODUCTION, that unit produces 1 additional hit; that hit must be used to produce fighters or infantry.*

- +1 free fighter or infantry per production
- Critical for Liberate invasions (need infantry equal to planet resources)
